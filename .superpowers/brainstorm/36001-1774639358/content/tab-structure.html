Tab Structure Options

iOS 26's floating tab bar works best with 3–5 focused tabs. Here are three ways to organize your features.

A

4 Tabs — Merge Meals + Recipes

Recipes become a section within Meals (or vice versa). These are tightly coupled — you pick a recipe to plan a meal.

3 Tabs — Dashboard absorbs Calendar

The Dashboard already shows today's chores and meals. Calendar becomes a view within Home (toggle between "Today" and "Month" views).

Home tab has a segmented control or scope bar: "Today" (dashboard) / "Calendar" (month view). Fewer tabs = cleaner bar, but Home does more.
C

4 Tabs — Merge Dashboard + Calendar, keep Recipes separate

Home becomes a unified timeline/calendar view with today's summary at top. Recipes stays its own tab since browsing recipes is a distinct activity.

Context

In all options, Profile/Settings moves to a toolbar button or a settings gear — it doesn't need a tab. iOS 26's tab bar is floating and translucent, so fewer tabs = more elegant.
